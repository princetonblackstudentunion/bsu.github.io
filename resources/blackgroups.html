Princeton African Students Association (PASA)

PASA raises awareness on Africa & Africa-related issues and explores/celebrates the diversity of African cultures in order to foster unity/friendship amongst members.

Religious Life at Princeton

The Office of Religious Life (ORL) located in Murray-Dodge Hall is the branch that support the many religious and secular communities on campus. On their website clubs, places, and resources relating to religion are listed.

Nearby Mosques: Islamic Circle of Mercer County, New Brunswick Islamic Center, Islamic Center of Ewing, Masjidul Taqwa, Masjid As-Saffat

Nearby Churches: Change Church (Formerly Kingdom Church), Worship Church, Jacob’s Well, Princeton United Methodist Church, Princeton University Chapel, Nassau Christian Center Assembly Gospel, St. Paul’s Roman Catholic Cathedral

Nerby Synagogues: Jewish Center of Princeton, Congregation Beth Chaim, Adath Israel Congregation

Princeton Caribbean Connection (PCC)

PCC aims to provide all Princeton students a means for celebrating and learning about the Caribbean.

Princeton Association of Black Women (PABW)

PABW celebrates, unifies and educates the community of black women on the Princeton campus.

Order of Black Male Excellence (OBME)

OBME promotes and enhances the Black male experience on campus through a range of academic, social and service events.

National Society of Black Engineers (NSBE)

NSBE promotes academic excellence through the form of tutoring, mentoring and incentive programs

Princeton Ethiopian and Eritrean Students Association (PEESA)

Dedicated to bringing some of Ethiopia and Eritrea to Princeton through various cultural, educational, as well as service-oriented events.

Black Pre-Med Society of Princeton

The Black Pre-medical Society of Princeton University seeks to empower the Black premedical community to tackle the unique issues that face underrepresented students in medicine.
